GartenApp: Introduction

Background

My name is Erik Hempelmann, and I am currently an 8th semester Master's student in Applied Biology at Hochschule Bremen, programmer, product manager, and autodidact. My academic journey began in 2016 when I embarked on my Biology studies with a growing interest in data analytics related to biological sciences.

During my undergraduate studies, I undertook a significant challenge: my bachelor's thesis focused on a comprehensive big data analysis comparing the DNA sequences of Daphnia magna and Daphnia pulex globin species. This six-month endeavor painfully highlighted the inadequacies of my academic preparation for such a complex task. Despite the struggles, including considerable efforts with Microsoft Excel, I managed to produce a thesis that, while not stellar, was coherent and sufficient to earn my degree. This experience left me determined to find more capable tools for managing similar data-intensive tasks.

Unable to meet the grade requirements for bioinformatics courses, I pursued an alternative path and applied to the ISTAB Masterâ€™s program at Hochschule Bremen. This program offered a flexible curriculum with a strong emphasis on statistics in its first semester, which turned out to be enlightening. Under the guidance of Dr. Robert Taube, I was introduced to the world of programming through a project that involved writing a small statistics script in R. This experience opened my eyes to the vast possibilities that programming languages offer.

The COVID-19 pandemic, while a period of global difficulty, provided me with the time to deepen my programming skills. I transitioned from R to writing solutions in Rust, a lower-level programming language, allowing me to develop several functional programs, including a blockchain trading bot and an evaluation program using performance metrics. My academic and practical experience further expanded when I tackled an analytics and visualization project for FTIR spectroscopy, which introduced me to Python, adding another powerful tool to my repertoire.

Career Development

Simultaneously, I began working with a biogas plant operator consulting agency and specialized in energy consulting. This position enabled me to develop an automation application tailored to energy management, which proved to be a financial success for the company. Upon completion of the application, I was offered a role as a product manager at the company's headquarters. Due to familial responsibilities and financial pressure I accepted this offer.

Upon completing my last course before my Master's degree in March 2023, I reconnected with Dr. Robert Taube to discuss the possibility of writing my master's thesis under his tutelage. We explored the potential of developing a mobile or web application designed to gather data on crop horticulture. Our aim was to provide users with a sophisticated optimization model for crop rotation schemes.
